How the Snipe System Works

A plain-English walkthrough of how osu!friends detects rival plays, triggers challenges, and confirms snipes.

1 The Big Picture

Every user on osu!friends can add up to 3 rivals. Once rivals are set, a background cron job runs periodically and does three things in order:

Phase 1 — Detect new rival plays
Fetch each rival's recent top plays from the osu! API. If a play is significant and new, notify the watcher and open a snipe challenge.
Phase 2 — Check open challenges
For every open snipe challenge, check whether the watcher has beaten the target score on that specific map yet.
Phase 3 — Expire old challenges
Any challenge older than 7 days that hasn't been sniped is marked expired. The window has closed.

2 How We Get the "Latest Play"

We don't watch a rival's recent activity feed — we pull their top 50 best plays from the osu! API for their chosen game mode, then filter down to what's new and significant.

What we fetch

GET /users/{osuId}/scores/best?mode={mode}&limit=50

This returns the rival's 50 highest-pp scores of all time. We look at when each score was set to find plays from the last 48 hours.

Why top plays, not recent activity? The osu! API's recent activity endpoint doesn't include pp values reliably for all modes. Top plays always have clean pp data and are the scores rivals are proudest of — exactly the ones worth sniping.

3 What Counts as a "Significant" Play

Not every score triggers a notification. A play must pass two filters:

Filter 1 — Recency

The score must have been set within the last 48 hours. Anything older is ignored.

Filter 2 — Significance threshold (dynamic)

The play's pp must be above a percentage of the rival's average top-play pp. The threshold scales based on their skill level — lower-ranked players set "significant" plays constantly, so we raise the bar to avoid spam.

Rival's avg pp Play must be ≥ Example
Under 100pp 97% of avg Avg 80pp → play needs 77.6pp+
100 – 299pp 92% of avg Avg 200pp → play needs 184pp+
300 – 499pp 85% of avg Avg 400pp → play needs 340pp+
500 – 999pp 80% of avg Avg 700pp → play needs 560pp+
1000pp+ 75% of avg Avg 1200pp → play needs 900pp+

We also track every play we've already notified about per watcher, so you never get two notifications for the same score even if the cron runs again before the watcher checks.

4 What Happens When a Play Is Found

When a new significant play passes both filters, three things fire simultaneously:

① In-app notification

The watcher gets a bell notification inside osu!friends: "Rival hit a 342pp play! — Map Name [Hard] — Want to snipe it?" with a direct link to the beatmap.

② osu! in-game DM

Our osu! bot sends a direct message to the watcher inside the game itself. They don't need to have osu!friends open to find out.

③ SnipeChallenge created

A challenge record is saved to the database containing the beatmap ID, the rival's pp on that map, and a 7-day expiry clock. This is what Phase 2 monitors.

5 What Counts as a Snipe

On each subsequent cron run, Phase 2 checks every open challenge:

The check

We call GET /beatmaps/{beatmapId}/scores/users/{watcherOsuId} — this returns the watcher's personal best score on that specific map.

If watcher's pp > rival's target pp → Snipe confirmed.
If not → challenge stays open until it expires.

Important: it's a pp comparison on the same beatmap, not a leaderboard rank comparison. You just need to post a better pp score than your rival did on that map within 7 days.

When a snipe is confirmed, both players get notified:

  • Watcher gets: "Snipe confirmed! You beat [rival]'s score on [map]."
  • Rival gets an in-app notification and an osu! DM: "[watcher] just sniped your score!"

Challenge States

OPEN — waiting, 7-day window still active
SNIPED — watcher beat the target pp
EXPIRED — 7 days passed, no snipe

Monthly snipe counts on the profile and leaderboard only count SNIPED challenges. Expired challenges count for neither player.
